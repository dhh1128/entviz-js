## Executive summary

Overall posture is solid for a port at this milestone: the JS implementation is structurally correct (escaping, determinism within-process, palette/Oklab logic all sound), and most findings are test-coverage gaps, missing comments, and CI/release hardening rather than behavioral defects. The one milestone-blocking correctness issue is SPEC-F1 (HIGH/CONFIRMED): a mixed-case Ethereum address that fails EIP-55 is a spec MUST-reject but, with no ported eth parser, falls through to the UTF-8->base64url fallback and renders a plausible-but-wrong SVG instead of being refused — a scope gap that actually changes rendered output. The remaining HIGH recommend-fix items are guardrail gaps, not live bugs: no CVD test suite (PSY-JS-F1), release.yml skipping the npm-audit and Tier-A conformance gates (OPS-F1), zero coverage on cli.ts/Entviz.tsx (TST-F1), no committed golden-SVG determinism gate (TST-F2), LIB_VERSION drifting from package.json on every release (MNT-F1/SPEC-F2), and three load-bearing invariants (hash-text-not-bytes, black-never-background, domain-tag freeze) carrying no callsite comments (MNT-F2/MNT-F3/MNT-F4). The single most urgent action is to fix SPEC-F1 — reject bad-EIP-55 addresses at parse time so a corrupted address fails closed rather than rendering — and immediately follow it by wiring the audit + Tier-A conformance gates into release.yml (OPS-F1) so the release path can't ship around the spec checks.
