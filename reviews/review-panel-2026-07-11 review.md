## Executive summary

The panel surfaced 25 findings with no CRITICALs but 9 HIGHs, all recommend-fix and all high-confidence — the milestone is blocked primarily by a cluster of consumer-facing and accessibility defects rather than by any single catastrophic bug. The most urgent action is fixing the stale documentation (DX-F1): every consumer-facing doc still advertises spec v11 with CESR/SSH/bech32 unported, while the code is actually at v15 with all 22 parsers shipping — so consumers will wrongly conclude the library cannot handle inputs it fully supports. Other HIGH blockers cluster into three themes: React correctness (CRAFT-F2 stale-closure emit across all three complex components), accessibility contract violations on the pill popover (A11Y-F1 no focus-on-open, A11Y-F2 missing aria-modal), and error/i18n quality (ERR-F1 uncoded errors, ERR-F2 wrong-operation English fragment, L10N-F1 RTL detection broken via isRtlLocale("")). The two structural HIGHs — CRAFT-F1 (2207-line monolith) and DX-F2 (no bundler guidance for the raw-.ts shipping model) — are lower-risk but high-friction. Most fixes are small and API-preserving; the near-term plan is a doc-truth sweep plus the small A11Y/L10N/error one-liners, deferring only the M2b/M3 localization items (L10N-F4, L10N-F5) and the checksum-echo hardening (ERR-F5).
